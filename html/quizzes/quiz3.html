Quiz #3: Delay, Flip Flops, & Registers

EGCP 1010: Digital Logic Design

Quiz Information

This quiz tests your knowledge of advanced circuitry and components, and how they work. Familiarize yourself with delay/feedback, the SR latch, D/JK flip flops, and registers.

Study Guide

Concept of Delay and Feedback

  • Be prepared to complete a waveform given inputs and delay amounts.
  • Understand the differences between stable (memory) and unstable (oscillatory) feedback.

Basic Functions of the SR Latch

  • Be prepared to complete a waveform given inputs to an SR latch.
  • Know the commands an SR Latch (Hold Set Reset & Not Allowed) can perform.
  • Understand what happens when the not allowed state is entered and exited.

Understand the D and JK Flip Flop

  • Be prepared to complete a waveform given inputs.
  • Know the commands a D FF (LOAD DATA) can perform.
  • Know the commands a JK FF (Hold Set Reset & Toggle) can perform.
  • Be able to write out the characteristic equations for Q+ for Both D and JK flip-flops.
  • Know the definitions on the Memory definition sheet - things like Rising edge triggered, Next State, Setup time, Hold time, etc.

Key Concepts of Registers

  • Definition of a register & concept of shifting left or right, and what a circular shift is.
  • Given a set of Flip Flops and interconnected Muxes, construct the command table.
  • Given the 4 bit shift register and a set of inputs (table or waveform) show next state result.
