Test #1

EGCP 1010: Digital Logic Design

Test Information

This test assesses your knowledge of digital logic, as well as your ability to perform arithmetic in different bases and analyze circuits. This test will cover chapters 1-5, 7 & 9 of the textbook.

Study Guide

Topic / Skill Example Task
Convert numbers between base 10-2-8-16 & understand positional numbers. Convert 123.75₁₀ to base 8, 2, and 16.
Perform Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication & Division in Binary. Calculate 10100 (+, -, *, /) 01101.
Given a schematic diagram, provide the Boolean Algebra.
Given the Boolean Algebra, provide the schematic diagram. Draw the schematic for F = (A’B + C(AD)’).
Using the Laws of Boolean Algebra, simplify a Boolean expression. Simplify F = AB’ + AC’ + B(AC) + C’.
Use DeMorgan’s Theorem to find the inverse of a function F. Given F = A’B + C(A + DB), find F’.
Given a Truth Table, find a Minterm expansion in Algebra and list forms. (See examples from class/textbook)
Given one shorthand standard form, find the other three forms. Given F(X,Y,Z) = ΠM(0,1,3,6) • ΠD(4), find F minterm, F’ minterm, F’ Maxterm.
Plot a minterm list or truth table expression onto a K-map. Plot F(W,X,Y,Z) = Σ(0,1,2,6,8,11,13,15) + d(4,12) onto a 4-variable K-map.
From a K-Map, find the Min 2-level SOP and POS solutions and identify EPITs. Find the Min SOP and POS solutions for the function F above.
Find multi-level networks by factoring an SOP or multiplying a POS. Find and show the cost of multi-level solutions for both SOP and POS forms from the previous step.
Convert SOP to NAND-NAND and POS to NOR-NOR networks. Draw schematics of your solutions and convert them.
Understand how multiple output problems are globally minimized by sharing terms. (Conceptual - e.g., BCD to Seven Segment Problem)
Use Multiplexers, Tri-State Buffers, and Decoders to implement logic. Implement F(X,Y,Z) = Σm(0,1,3,6) using a Mux or Decoder.
Understand the function and implementation of Programmable Logic Devices (PLD). Know acronyms (PLD, PLA, CPLD, FPGA) and read a programmed PLD schematic.
Understand the function of a ROM and how it is sized. For a 16 Meg by 8 ROM, how many address lines? How many data lines?
